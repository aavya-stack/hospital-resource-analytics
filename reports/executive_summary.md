# Executive Summary: Hospital Resource Reallocation & Capacity Optimization

## Business Problem
Hospital leadership needs to reduce avoidable patient waiting time, improve bed and ICU utilization, and reallocate scarce staff and diagnostic resources across departments without compromising care quality.

## Key Findings
- Total patient records analyzed: 12,000
- Average wait time: 98.0 minutes
- Average bed occupancy: 79.8%
- Average ICU utilization: 27.6%
- 30-day readmission rate: 8.1%
- Emergency cases represent 24.4% of patient volume
- Over-utilized departments: Emergency, General Medicine, ICU
- Under-utilized departments: Maternity

## Strategic Recommendations
- **High | ICU**: Move 5 nurses from Maternity to ICU during evening and weekend peaks. Expected 10-18% wait-time reduction and improved throughput during surge windows.
- **High | ICU**: Create a flexible surge pool of 8 beds for ICU and adjacent high-acuity pathways. Expected 6-12% improvement in bed availability and lower left-without-being-seen risk.
- **High | Emergency**: Move 4 nurses from Maternity to Emergency during evening and weekend peaks. Expected 10-18% wait-time reduction and improved throughput during surge windows.
- **High | Emergency**: Create a flexible surge pool of 8 beds for Emergency and adjacent high-acuity pathways. Expected 6-12% improvement in bed availability and lower left-without-being-seen risk.
- **High | General Medicine**: Move 4 nurses from Maternity to General Medicine during evening and weekend peaks. Expected 10-18% wait-time reduction and improved throughput during surge windows.
- **High | General Medicine**: Create a flexible surge pool of 7 beds for General Medicine and adjacent high-acuity pathways. Expected 6-12% improvement in bed availability and lower left-without-being-seen risk.
- **Medium | Emergency**: Add protected diagnostic blocks for Emergency and shift non-urgent MRI/CT to low-demand mornings. Expected 8-15% reduction in diagnostic turnaround time.
- **Medium | ICU**: Add protected diagnostic blocks for ICU and shift non-urgent MRI/CT to low-demand mornings. Expected 8-15% reduction in diagnostic turnaround time.

## Financial Impact Estimate
Reducing average wait time by 10-18% can improve throughput, reduce overtime, and recover capacity leakage. A conservative 5% reduction in avoidable length-of-stay pressure and diagnostic delays can create meaningful bed-day capacity without major capital expansion.

## Operating Model
Implement a weekly capacity control tower, flexible staffing pools, weekend surge protocols, and diagnostic slot governance. Refresh the model monthly with updated admissions, staffing, equipment, and discharge data.
